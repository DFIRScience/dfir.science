This article will look examine the term cybercrime, and give an overview of 'cybercrime technologies' are.

First, the terms 'cybercrime' and 'digital crime' are often used interchangeably. We can break 'cybercrime' down into 'cyber', meaning "computer" or "computer network", and  "crime" meaning "an action or an instance of negligence that is deemed injurious to the public welfare or morals or to the interests of the state and that is legally prohibited".

Based on these definitions, the definition of 'cybercrime' can be "criminal activity or a crime that involves the Internet, a computer system, or computer technology".

This definition makes sense, but when I hear 'cyber', I normally assume a network like, for example, the Internet (cyberspace). I am unaware of any term with 'cyber' as a prefix to describe a stand-alone computer. You could potentially have any cyber-noun, but 'cybercomputer' just doesn't sound quite right; a little redundant, but not completely. Then again, 'cyber' by itself does not (in my mind) conjure up images of a stand-alone computer. To me, 'cyber' implies some sort of connection, communication, and/or information sharing (beyond sneakernet) And this is how I differentiate between cybercrime and digital crime. I see digital crime as a more general term than cybercrime.

To me, if a crime has a primarily digital component, it may be classified as a digital crime. For example, credit card skimmers that have no networking capabilities store all the data to a local disk. To me, this would be a digital crime because the crime is primarily facilitated by a digital device. However, there is no communication with any other device; no 'cyber' component.

If we take the same skimmer, and give it some way to connect to a network and upload data, now there is a 'cyber' component. It is still a digital crime, but now because there is a connection and communication, it may also be classified as a cybercrime. A cybercrime, however, must be a digital crime.

Now, what are cybercrime technologies?

If cybercrime is a criminal activity or a crime that involves the Internet or a transfer of data, then cybercrime technologies are technologies that facilitate the activity, such as the collection and transfer of data that is illegal to collect or transport.

When I think of cybercrime technologies, I think of malicious software, platforms and frameworks. Crimeware like ZeuS and SpyEye come to mind, as does frameworks like metasploit (even though it is a security kit). Anti-forensic could also be included, since they may be used to hide traces of cybercrime. Basically any technology that facilitates the perpetration of cybercrime.
